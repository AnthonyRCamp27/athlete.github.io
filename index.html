My Story

I was born in St. Louis and raised between East St. Louis and Belleville, Illinois, where pride, resilience, and a championship mindset are part of the culture. That environment shaped my work ethic and competitive drive, and I bring that same mentality onto the runway every time I compete.

I began my college career at the junior college level as a 22-foot long jumper and 6-foot high jumper. In two seasons, I developed into a national finalist, improving to 23 feet in the long jump and 6'8" in the high jump. After transferring to Division I, I continued that progression, becoming a conference champion and school record holder while developing into a consistent 25-foot long jumper and elite high jumper.

My career has been defined by steady progression, resilience, and the ability to elevate at each new level of competition. I am now consistently jumping 25 feet and positioning myself among the top long jumpers in the United States.

This year, I will be competing at major national-level meets and pursuing opportunities on bigger stages against elite fields. My goal is not to rank top 20 or top 10 in the U.S., but to compete for a top-three national standing and establish myself among the best in the world.
